The concept of the "Inner Citadel" is central to Stoic philosophy. It represents the impregnable fortress of the mind, a place of retreat where one can find peace and clarity regardless of external circumstances.

<Blockquote variant="default">
  "You have power over your mind - not outside events. Realize this, and you will find strength." — Marcus Aurelius
</Blockquote>

## Building the Citadel

Constructing this inner fortress requires discipline and practice. It involves recognizing the dichotomy of control: distinguishing between what is within our power (our thoughts, beliefs, and actions) and what is not (other people's opinions, the weather, the economy).

By focusing our energy solely on what we can control, we fortify our inner citadel against the siege of anxiety and frustration.
