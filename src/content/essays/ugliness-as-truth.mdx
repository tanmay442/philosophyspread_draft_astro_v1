The classical world equated beauty with truth and goodness. But what if truth is inherently ugly? What if the chaotic, asymmetrical, and disturbing aspects of reality are more authentic than the polished ideals of classical art?

<Blockquote variant="warning">
  "We have art in order not to die of the truth." — Friedrich Nietzsche
</Blockquote>

## Embracing the Dionysian

Nietzsche argued for a balance between the Apollonian (order, reason, symmetry) and the Dionysian (chaos, emotion, intoxication). By suppressing the Dionysian, we deny a fundamental part of human existence. True art must embrace the ugliness of reality to achieve profound resonance.
