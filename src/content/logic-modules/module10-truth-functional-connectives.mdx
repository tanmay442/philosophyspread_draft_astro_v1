Truth-functional connectives are logical operators that determine the truth value of a compound statement based on the truth values of its components.

## The Five Basic Connectives

| Symbol | Name | Natural Language |
|--------|------|------------------|
| ~ | Negation | "not" |
| • | Conjunction | "and" |
| v | Disjunction | "or" |
| ⊃ | Conditional | "if...then" |
| ≡ | Biconditional | "if and only if" |

## Logical Equivalences

Many connectives can be defined in terms of others:

<CodeBlock code={`P v Q = ~((~P)•(~Q))
P ⊃ Q = ~P v Q
P ≡ Q = (P ⊃ Q)•(Q ⊃ P)`} />
