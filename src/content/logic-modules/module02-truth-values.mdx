In classical logic, every proposition has exactly one truth value: it is either true (T) or false (F). This is known as the principle of bivalence.

## Logical Connectives

We use logical connectives to build complex propositions from simpler ones. The truth value of a complex proposition depends entirely on the truth values of its parts.

### Conjunction (AND)

A conjunction is true if and only if both of its conjuncts are true.

A disjunction is true if at least one of its disjuncts is true.
