## 1. Beyond Truth Tables: The Need for Proofs

In the previous modules, you learned how truth tables can be used to determine the validity of any deductive argument. However, for an argument with five or more simple statements, a truth table would require 32 rows or more. This is not only tedious but impractical.

Instead, we can prove an argument's validity by deducing its conclusion from its premises in a series of short, simple steps. This is the **method of deduction**.

## 3. The First 9 Tools: Rules of Inference

The **Rules of Inference** are the fundamental, valid moves you are allowed to make.

  <div class="bg-surface-container-low border-2 border-secondary p-4">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-lg mb-2">1. Modus Ponens (M.P.)</h4>
    <pre class="bg-surface-container p-2 font-mono text-sm">If P then Q
P
 therefore Q</pre>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-surface-container-low border-2 border-secondary p-4">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-lg mb-2">2. Modus Tollens (M.T.)</h4>
    <pre class="bg-surface-container p-2 font-mono text-sm">If P then Q
Not Q
 therefore Not P</pre>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-surface-container-low border-2 border-secondary p-4">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-lg mb-2">3. Hypothetical Syllogism (H.S.)</h4>
    <pre class="bg-surface-container p-2 font-mono text-sm">If P then Q
If Q then R
 therefore If P then R</pre>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-surface-container-low border-2 border-secondary p-4">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-lg mb-2">4. Disjunctive Syllogism (D.S.)</h4>
    <pre class="bg-surface-container p-2 font-mono text-sm">P or Q
Not P
 therefore Q</pre>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-surface-container-low border-2 border-secondary p-4">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-lg mb-2">5. Constructive Dilemma (C.D.)</h4>
    <pre class="bg-surface-container p-2 font-mono text-sm">(If P then Q) and (If R then S)
P or R
 therefore Q or S</pre>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-surface-container-low border-2 border-secondary p-4">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-lg mb-2">6. Simplification (Simp.)</h4>
    <pre class="bg-surface-container p-2 font-mono text-sm">P and Q
 therefore P</pre>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-surface-container-low border-2 border-secondary p-4">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-lg mb-2">7. Conjunction (Conj.)</h4>
    <pre class="bg-surface-container p-2 font-mono text-sm">P

  </div>

## 4. Formal Proofs in Action

A **formal proof of validity** is a sequence of statements where each step is either a premise or follows from the preceding steps by one of the valid rules.

## 5. Rules of Replacement

The Rules of Replacement are based on logical equivalence.

<div class="space-y-6 my-8">
  <div>
    <h4 class="font-bold text-lg mb-2">1. De Morgan's Theorems (De M.)</h4>
    <pre class="bg-surface-container p-3 font-mono text-sm">~(P * Q) = (~P v ~Q)
~(P v Q) = (~P * ~Q)</pre>
  </div>
  <div>
    <h4 class="font-bold text-lg mb-2">2. Commutation (Com.)</h4>
    <pre class="bg-surface-container p-3 font-mono text-sm">(P v Q) = (Q v P)
(P * Q) = (Q * P)</pre>
  </div>
  <div>
    <h4 class="font-bold text-lg mb-2">3. Double Negation (D.N.)</h4>
    <pre class="bg-surface-container p-3 font-mono text-sm">P = ~~P</pre>
  </div>
  <div>
    <h4 class="font-bold text-lg mb-2">4. Transposition (Trans.)</h4>
    <pre class="bg-surface-container p-3 font-mono text-sm">(P - Q) = (~Q - ~P)</pre>
  </div>
  <div>
    <h4 class="font-bold text-lg mb-2">5. Material Implication (Impl.)</h4>
    <pre class="bg-surface-container p-3 font-mono text-sm">(P - Q) = (~P v Q)</pre>
  </div>
  <div>
    <h4 class="font-bold text-lg mb-2">6. Tautology (Taut.)</h4>
    <pre class="bg-surface-container p-3 font-mono text-sm">P = (P v P)
P = (P * P)</pre>
  </div>
</div>

## 6. Strategy and Technique

<ul class="list-disc pl-8 space-y-2 mt-6">
  <li><strong>Start Simple:</strong> Look for immediate sub-conclusions from premises.</li>
  <li><strong>Work Backwards:</strong> Look at your conclusion and work backwards.</li>
  <li><strong>Eliminate Mismatches:</strong> Remove letters in premises but not in conclusion.</li>
  <li><strong>Introduce What's Missing:</strong> Add letters in conclusion but not in premises.</li>
</ul>
